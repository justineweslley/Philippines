Banaue Rice Terraces

About Banaue Rice Terraces

The Banaue Rice Terraces, located in the mountains of Ifugao province in northern Luzon, are often referred to as the “Eighth Wonder of the World.” Carved by hand into the Cordillera mountain slopes more than 2,000 years ago, these terraces were built by the Ifugao people using only simple tools and indigenous engineering methods.

The terraces continue to be used for rice and vegetable farming today, sustained by a centuries-old irrigation system that channels water from nearby rainforests. They are a living cultural landscape — a testament to the skill, resilience, and tradition of the Ifugao communities who still inhabit the area.

  • Location: Ifugao, Cordillera Region, Luzon
  • Altitude: Up to 1,500 meters above sea level
  • Known For: 2,000-year-old hand-carved rice terraces
  • Best Time to Visit: April - May (green terraces) / June (harvest season)
